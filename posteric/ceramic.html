Ceramic capacitor

Ceramic capacitors are passive electronic components that use a ceramic material as the dielectric. They are widely used in electronic circuits for their small size, low cost, and ability to handle high frequencies. Ceramic capacitors are non-polarized, meaning they can be connected in either direction in a circuit. These capacitors are commonly used in filtering, bypassing, coupling, and decoupling applications due to their excellent high-frequency characteristics and low loss.

Specifications
Capacitance Range: Typically from a few picofarads (pF) to several microfarads (µF)
Voltage Rating: Ranges from 6.3V to 1000V or more
Tolerance: From ±1% to ±20% (depending on the application)
Temperature Coefficient (Dielectric Class): Class I (e.g., NP0/C0G) – Very stable, used in precision applications
Class II (e.g., X7R, X5R) – Moderate stability for general-purpose use
Class III (e.g., Y5V, Z5U) – Higher capacitance variation, for non-critical applications
ESR (Equivalent Series Resistance): Low ESR, typically in milliohms, ensuring good high-frequency performance.
Operating Temperature Range: Typically from -55°C to +125°C, depending on the dielectric material.
Military and Industrial Standards Military Standards:
MIL-PRF-55681: This U.S. military standard defines the performance of high-reliability ceramic capacitors.
MIL-PRF-123: Used for precision, high-reliability capacitors with stable temperature characteristics.
MIL-STD-202: Governs the testing methods, including environmental and mechanical testing for ceramic capacitors.
Industrial Standards:
AEC-Q200: Standard for automotive-grade capacitors ensuring high reliability in harsh environments.
IEC 60384-8/9: Specifies the requirements for fixed capacitors for use in electronic equipment.
EIA RS-198: Outlines the ceramic capacitor specifications and classifications based on size, performance, and dielectric type.
Packaging Types
Surface Mount Device (SMD) / Surface Mount Technology (SMT): Common package sizes include 0402, 0603, 0805, 1206, 1210, 1812 (EIA codes), ideal for automated assembly on printed circuit boards (PCBs).
Through-Hole: Radial or axial leaded configurations used in specific applications that require manual placement or sturdier mechanical fixing.
Bulk Packaging: Capacitors are supplied in bulk for general use, often in bags or boxes for hand assembly or prototyping.
Tape and Reel: Used for automated surface-mount assembly processes in high-volume manufacturing environments. Components are placed on a continuous strip of tape that feeds into pick-and-place machines.
Cut Tape/Ammo Pack: Packaging designed for through-hole components, where capacitors are packed in a cardboard "ammo" carrier for automated insertion.

Ceramic capacitors are vital in modern electronics, from consumer gadgets to industrial and military applications, due to their reliability and performance across a broad range of environments.
