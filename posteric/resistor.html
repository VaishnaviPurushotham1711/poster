Resistor

A resistor is a passive electronic component that opposes or limits the flow of electric current in a circuit. It is one of the most fundamental components in electronics, used to control voltage levels, divide voltages, and protect sensitive components by regulating current. The resistance value of a resistor is measured in ohms (Ω), and it follows Ohm’s Law (V = IR), which states that voltage (V) across the resistor is the product of the current (I) flowing through it and its resistance (R).
Resistors are classified based on materials, construction, and application requirements, with key characteristics like resistance value, power rating, tolerance, and temperature coefficient.
