Capacitors

A capacitor is an electronic component that stores electrical energy in an electric field between two conductive plates. The plates are separated by a dielectric material, which determines its capacitance. Capacitors are used for energy storage, filtering, and signal coupling in circuits. Their capacitance is measured in farads and depends on the plate size, separation, and dielectric material.
Capacitors come in various types, each designed to suit specific electrical requirements and applications.
The main types of capacitors include:

    Ceramic capacitors

    Electrolytic capacitor

    Tantalum capacitors

    Film capacitor

    Super capacitor
