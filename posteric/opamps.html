Operational amplifiers

An operational amplifier (op-amp) is a high-gain electronic voltage amplifier with differential input (two inputs, usually labeled inverting and non-inverting) and a single-ended output. Op-amps are fundamental building blocks in analog electronics, widely used for signal processing tasks such as amplification, filtering, and mathematical operations like addition, subtraction, integration, and differentiation. They are highly versatile and are employed in a range of applications, including audio equipment, control systems, sensors, and instrumentation.
An ideal op-amp has infinite input impedance, zero output impedance, infinite gain, and infinite bandwidth, though practical op-amps deviate from these ideal characteristics. Common performance metrics for real-world op-amps include gain bandwidth product (GBW), slew rate, input bias current, and offset voltage.
