Diodes

A diode is a semiconductor device that allows current to flow in one direction while blocking it in the opposite direction. Diodes have two terminals: the anode and the cathode. When the anode is at a higher voltage than the cathode, the diode conducts electricity (forward-biased). In reverse bias, the diode blocks current flow. Diodes are widely used in applications such as rectification (converting AC to DC), signal demodulation, voltage regulation, and protection circuits. They are essential components in modern electronics, offering control over the direction of current in a circuit.
