Zener diode

A Zener diode is a special type of diode that is designed to operate in the reverse breakdown region. Unlike standard diodes, which are damaged if reverse breakdown occurs, Zener diodes are specifically engineered to conduct in reverse once the applied voltage reaches a certain threshold, known as the Zener breakdown voltage. This property makes them ideal for voltage regulation in circuits, where they maintain a constant output voltage regardless of input fluctuations. Zener diodes are widely used for overvoltage protection, voltage reference circuits, and clamping applications.

Specifications
Zener Voltage (Vz): The reverse voltage at which the Zener diode begins to conduct. Common Zener voltages range from 2.4V to 200V, depending on the part number.
Power Dissipation (Pd): The maximum amount of power the diode can dissipate, typically from 500mW to 5W, depending on the size and package type.
Tolerance: The accuracy of the Zener voltage, usually specified as a percentage (e.g., ±5%, ±2%). Tighter tolerances are used in precision applications.
Maximum Reverse Current (Iz): The maximum current the diode can handle in reverse bias without damage. This is important when designing circuits to ensure safe operation.
Zener Impedance (Zz): The dynamic resistance of the Zener diode in reverse breakdown. A lower Zener impedance indicates better voltage regulation.
Temperature Coefficient: A measure of how the Zener voltage changes with temperature, typically given in mV/°C. Low-temperature coefficient diodes are used in temperature-sensitive applications.
Forward Voltage (Vf): The forward voltage drop of the Zener diode, typically 0.7V like standard diodes. Operating Temperature Range: Zener diodes can operate from -55°C to +200°C, depending on the specific diode, making them suitable for use in extreme environments.
Military and Industrial Standards
Military Standards:
MIL-PRF-19500: This standard specifies the general performance and reliability requirements for semiconductor diodes, including Zener diodes, used in military and aerospace systems.
MIL-STD-202: Establishes the environmental and mechanical testing procedures, such as thermal shock and vibration resistance, for ensuring the ruggedness of Zener diodes in military applications.
MIL-STD-750: Defines testing methods for semiconductor devices, including Zener diodes, covering electrical and thermal characteristics to ensure performance under extreme conditions.
Industrial Standards:
JEDEC JESD22: Outlines guidelines for stress testing, thermal cycling, and reliability testing for semiconductor components, including Zener diodes.
IEC 60747-2: This international standard governs the requirements for semiconductor devices, including Zener diodes, ensuring that they meet industrial safety and performance benchmarks.
AEC-Q101: A qualification standard for automotive electronic components, ensuring that Zener diodes used in automotive applications are reliable under high-stress environments.
RoHS & REACH Compliance: Ensures that the materials used in manufacturing Zener diodes are environmentally safe, restricting the use of hazardous substances such as lead.
Packaging Types
Axial Leaded (DO-35, DO-41): The most common form for through-hole applications, where the diode has leads extending from both sides of a cylindrical body. These are used in a wide variety of circuits, especially in power supplies and voltage regulators.
Surface Mount (SMD, SOT-23, SOD-123, SOD-323): Zener diodes in surface-mount packages are designed for automated assembly on high-density circuit boards. These packages save space and are widely used in consumer electronics, such as mobile devices, laptops, and other compact electronics.
Glass Encapsulated (MELF): For high reliability and resistance to environmental factors, glass-encapsulated Zener diodes offer better protection against moisture and temperature fluctuations. These are used in applications where longevity and robustness are critical.
Chip Arrays: Some Zener diodes come in multi-diode arrays for integrated circuits, where several diodes are housed in a single package. These arrays are commonly used in voltage regulation and protection circuits where multiple Zener diodes are needed.
Bulk Packaging: Zener diodes are available in bulk or tape-and-reel formats for automated high-volume production lines in industrial environments.
