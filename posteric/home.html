What is Electronics ?

Electronics is the science and technology concerned with the control and manipulation of electric current through various components to perform specific functions. It involves the study and application of circuits composed of passive elements (resistors, capacitors, inductors) and active components (transistors, diodes, integrated circuits) that work together to process, transmit, and store information. Electronics encompasses a wide range of systems, from simple analog devices like radios to complex digital systems such as microprocessors and communication networks. It is foundational to numerous industries, including telecommunications, computing, automotive, and medical technology, driving innovation in automation, smart devices, and digital infrastructure.Active devices and Passive devices are two main catogaries of electronics.

Understanding the difference between these components is essential for designing circuits and systems in electronics.

Active devices

Active devices are electronic components that rely on an external power source to control the flow of electrical current. These devices are capable of amplifying, switching, or modifying signals within a circuit.

Passive Devices

Passive devices are electronic components that do not generate energy but instead absorb, store, or dissipate it. They rely on external power sources to operate and do not have the ability to amplify or control the flow of electrical energy.
