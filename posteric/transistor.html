Transistor

A transistor is a semiconductor device used to amplify or switch electronic signals. It is one of the most fundamental components in modern electronics, enabling everything from simple amplifiers to complex processors. Transistors control current or voltage flow, acting as an on/off switch or a signal amplifier. They are widely used in analog and digital circuits, including power management, signal amplification, oscillators, and computer processors.
A transistor typically has three terminals:
Base (B): Controls the flow of current between the other two terminals.
Collector (C): Current flows into the collector.
Emitter (E): Current flows out of the emitter.
