Thick film resistor

Thick film resistors are a type of fixed resistor made by applying a thick layer of resistive material (typically a metal oxide or conductive paste) onto a ceramic substrate. The resistive layer is then patterned and fired at high temperatures to create the desired resistance value. These resistors are widely used due to their versatility, affordability, and ability to handle a wide range of resistance values, power ratings, and environmental conditions. They are found in consumer electronics, automotive systems, power supplies, and precision industrial applications.
Compared to thin film resistors, thick film resistors are more cost-effective but generally offer lower precision and stability. However, they are still reliable for many general-purpose applications and have good performance in harsh conditions.

Specifications
Resistance Range: From 1Ω to several megaohms (MΩ), providing a broad range for general-purpose applications. Power Rating: Thick film resistors typically have power ratings from 1/16W to 2W. Higher power versions are available but less common.
Tolerance: Standard tolerance values range from ±1% to ±10%, though some precision versions offer tolerances as low as ±0.5%.
Temperature Coefficient of Resistance (TCR): The TCR of thick film resistors ranges from ±100 ppm/°C to ±250 ppm/°C. This makes them less temperature-stable than thin-film or wirewound resistors, but sufficient for many applications.
Operating Temperature Range: Thick film resistors typically operate between -55°C and +155°C, with some versions rated for even higher temperatures.
Voltage Rating: The maximum voltage these resistors can withstand varies with size and material but typically ranges from 50V to several hundred volts.
Military and Industrial Standards
Military Standards:
MIL-PRF-55342: Covers the requirements for precision thick film resistors for military applications. It specifies testing for tolerance, TCR, temperature cycling, and vibration resistance.
MIL-STD-202: Specifies environmental testing methods, including temperature cycling, mechanical shock, and vibration, to ensure durability and performance in extreme conditions.
MIL-STD-883: Defines reliability testing for microelectronics, including thick film resistors, ensuring performance under extreme conditions like radiation and thermal shock.
Industrial Standards:
IEC 60115-8: International standard for fixed resistors, including thick film resistors, detailing their construction, testing methods, and performance specifications.
JEDEC Standards: Ensures consistency in quality and reliability testing for thick film resistors in industrial electronics applications.
RoHS & REACH Compliance: Thick film resistors are typically RoHS-compliant, meaning they do not contain hazardous substances like lead, ensuring they meet environmental safety standards.
Packaging Types
Thick film resistors are available in various packaging types, particularly for surface-mount technology (SMT) and through-hole applications:
Surface-Mount Packages (SMD): 0805, 0603, 1206, and 0402: Common SMD sizes for thick film resistors, depending on the power rating and application. These resistors are suitable for automated assembly processes and compact circuit designs. Power Ratings: From 1/16W to 1W, depending on the package size. High-Power SMD: Larger surface-mount packages, such as 2512, are used for high-power thick film resistors, suitable for power circuits and current-sensing applications.
Through-Hole Packages: Axial Leaded: Thick film resistors are also available in cylindrical through-hole packages with axial leads, although this is less common than surface-mount options. Radial Leaded: Some specialized thick film resistors may also come in radial packages for specific applications.
Chip Arrays: Resistor Networks/Arrays: Thick film resistors can also be packaged as resistor arrays, where multiple resistors are integrated into a single chip. This is useful for space-constrained designs that require multiple resistance values in close proximity.

Thick film resistors offer a balance of performance, cost, and reliability, making them ideal for general-purpose, automotive, and industrial applications. Available in a variety of surface-mount and through-hole packages, these resistors meet industry and military standards for quality and durability in harsh environments. Although not as precise as thin film or wirewound resistors, they provide good performance in applications where precision is not the highest priority.
