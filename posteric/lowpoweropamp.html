Low-Power Op-Amps

Low-power operational amplifiers are designed specifically for applications where energy efficiency is crucial, such as battery-operated devices, portable electronics, and energy-sensitive systems. These op-amps operate with reduced power consumption while maintaining acceptable performance levels for a variety of tasks, including amplification, filtering, and signal conditioning.
Low-power op-amps typically feature lower supply voltage requirements and optimized designs that minimize quiescent current without significantly compromising performance parameters like gain, bandwidth, and noise. They are particularly beneficial in applications that prioritize longer battery life and lower heat generation.
Part Number Example
TLV2372 (Texas Instruments)
Description: A low-power CMOS operational amplifier designed for battery-operated and low-voltage applications.
Specifications:
Supply Voltage: 2.7V to 5.5V (single supply)
Quiescent Current: 15 µA per channel (typical)
Input Offset Voltage: 0.5 mV (maximum)
Gain Bandwidth Product: 1 MHz
Slew Rate: 0.5 V/µs
Input Impedance: 10 MΩ
Output Impedance: 50 Ω
Package Type: MSOP-8, SOIC-8, TSSOP-8, and others.
Specifications
Voltage Supply Range: Typically operates between 2.7V and 5.5V, making it suitable for low-voltage applications.
Quiescent Current: Low quiescent current, usually in the range of 15 µA, allowing for extended battery life in portable devices.
Open-Loop Gain: Moderate open-loop gain, around 100 dB at low frequencies, ensuring adequate amplification of input signals.
Common-Mode Rejection Ratio (CMRR): Approximately 90 dB, providing decent performance in rejecting common-mode signals.
Power Supply Rejection Ratio (PSRR): About 80 dB, maintaining stable performance with variations in supply voltage.
Temperature Range: Standard op-amps typically operate from -40°C to +85°C, with some versions rated for extended ranges.
Military and Industrial Standards
Military Standards:
MIL-PRF-38534: Specifies requirements for hybrid microcircuits, including low-power op-amps for military applications.
MIL-STD-883: Defines test methods for microelectronic devices, including reliability testing for low-power op-amps in military settings.
MIL-PRF-55342: Provides specifications for precision op-amps that can be utilized in military and aerospace applications.
Industrial Standards:
IEC 60115-8: Sets standards for fixed resistors, applicable to the resistive components within low-power op-amps.
JEDEC Standards: Guidelines for reliability and performance testing of electronic components, including low-power op-amps.
RoHS Compliance: Ensures that the op-amps do not contain hazardous substances, complying with regulations for electronic components in industrial applications.
Packaging Types
Low-power op-amps come in various packaging types to suit different applications:
DIP (Dual In-line Package): The traditional through-hole package with pins for insertion into a PCB, commonly used in prototyping and educational settings. Example: TLV2372 in an 8-DIP package.
SOIC (Small Outline Integrated Circuit): A surface-mount package that is smaller and allows for denser PCB layouts compared to DIP packages. Example: TLV2372 available in SOIC-8.
TSSOP (Thin Shrink Small Outline Package): A thinner version of the SOIC that provides even more compact designs, suitable for high-density applications. Example: TLV2372 available in TSSOP-8.
MSOP (Micro Small Outline Package): A compact surface-mount package for applications where space is limited, commonly used in low-power designs. Example: TLV2372 in MSOP-8 configuration.

Low-power operational amplifiers, such as the TLV2372, are crucial components for energy-efficient electronic applications. Their design focuses on minimizing power consumption while maintaining adequate performance for various signal processing tasks. Available in multiple packaging types and compliant with military and industrial standards, these op-amps provide reliable solutions for battery-operated devices, portable electronics, and other applications where power efficiency is a priority.
